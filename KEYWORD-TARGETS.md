# TaxPreparerTools.com — Realistic Keyword Targets

You asked which terms you can actually rank for. This is that list — winnable queries mapped to the pages you already have, in priority tiers, so you measure against targets you can hit instead of head terms owned by Intuit and the IRS.

**The one-line strategy:** a new, low-authority site wins on the *long tail* — specific IRS codes, specific 2026 figures, and specific tool/sub-questions — not head terms. Build a beachhead there, earn links and trust, then move up. Volume per term is modest, but these add up, they convert (high intent), and they're actually rankable in weeks-to-months, not years.

**How "winnable" was judged:** I checked live SERPs. "Tax preparer tools" is wall-to-wall billion-dollar suites (Intuit, Wolters Kluwer, Drake) — unwinnable for now. But "code 570 tax transcript" is forum posts and mid-tier tax-firm blogs — no giants, very beatable. Your decoder/code pages sit in that gap.

## How to track progress (Google Search Console)

- **Performance → Queries:** filter to the Tier 1 terms. Watch two numbers — *impressions* (are you being shown at all?) and *average position*. Anything at position **8-20** is "almost there"; a few links or a title tweak pushes it onto page one.
- **Sort by impressions** to find queries you're *already* appearing for but didn't target — those are the fastest wins (often Tier-1 code/figure variants).
- **The right scoreboard:** total impressions, number of Tier-1 queries on page one, and referring domains — *not* your rank for "tax preparer tools."
- **Realistic timeline:** Tier 1 in weeks-2 months after indexing, Tier 2 in 2-6 months, Tier 3 in 6-12+ alongside the link-building in `DISTRIBUTION-KIT.md`.
