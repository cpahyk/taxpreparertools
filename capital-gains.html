Understanding Capital Gains Tax
Short-Term vs Long-Term
Assets held 12 months or less are short-term and taxed at your ordinary income rate (up to 37%). Assets held more than 12 months qualify for preferential long-term rates: 0%, 15%, or 20% depending on income.
The Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT)
High-income taxpayers pay an additional 3.8% NIIT on the lesser of net investment income or the amount by which MAGI exceeds $200k (single) / $250k (MFJ). This applies to capital gains, dividends, interest, and passive rental income.
Capital Loss Harvesting
Realized losses offset gains dollar-for-dollar. Net losses up to $3,000 can offset ordinary income. Excess losses carry forward indefinitely. Beware the wash-sale rule: you cannot repurchase the same or substantially identical security within 30 days of the sale.

💡 Tax-Saving Strategies
📅
Hold 12+ months before selling to convert ST gains to LT gains — can cut your rate dramatically.
📉
Harvest losses before Dec 31 to offset gains. Use ETFs to maintain market exposure while avoiding wash-sale rules.
🎁
Gift appreciated stock to charity directly instead of selling — avoids capital gains entirely (IRS Form 8283).
🏦
Opportunity Zones (OZ): Defer and reduce gains by investing in qualified opportunity zone funds within 180 days.
