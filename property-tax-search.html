How this tool works

There's no national property tax database in the US — every county runs its own portal. This tool is a smart router: it gets you to the right one in three clicks.

STEP 01
Pick the state
All 50 states plus DC are covered. Some states like Connecticut, Massachusetts, and Maine assess property at the city/town level, not county — we flag that in the notes.
STEP 02
Pick the county
300+ counties have direct links to their assessor or tax-collector portal. If a county isn't listed, the Google fallback gets you there in one click.
STEP 03
Search the records
Most portals support address, parcel/PIN, or owner-name searches. We tell you which the county supports and any access fees, then send you straight to the search page.
Why we don't pull data ourselves

There is no single API or database that covers all 3,000+ US counties. Commercial aggregators like ATTOM and CoreLogic charge hundreds of dollars per month and still miss small counties.

Instead, we maintain a curated directory of official county portals. The data you see is the same data your CPA or attorney accesses — pulled live from the source of truth, with no middleman fees and no stale records.

For your privacy: we never see your search query. The handoff happens in your browser.
